Abstract
Robotic ball sports demonstrate the ability of robots to perform highly dynamic tasks. While most studies have focused on table tennis and soccer, real-time motion control in badminton remains underexplored. As the fastest racket sport, badminton demands more precise trajectory prediction, real-time motion planning, and higher dynamic responses of robotic arms, imposing significant challenges. This work presents a hybrid framework and a robot-badminton system for real-time shuttlecock striking. The framework (SL-IRRL) combines a supervised learning network for trajectory prediction with a two-stage Imitation-Relaxation training strategy for arm control, improving landing accuracy and convergence speed. Implemented on a robotic badminton system, our approach achieves a 90\% hitting success rate and a 70\% landing success rate over 60 trials, enabling sustained human-robot rallies. Comparative experiments with a UR5 arm validate the framework’s generalization and underscore the importance of high dynamic response of robotic arm in ball sports.
